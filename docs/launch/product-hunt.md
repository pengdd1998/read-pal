# Product Hunt Launch Copy

## Tagline
Your AI friend who reads with you — and remembers everything

## Description

read-pal is the reading companion for people who actually read (not just collect books).

Upload any EPUB and get:
- An AI partner who knows the book and discusses it with you in real-time
- Smart highlights and notes that connect across chapters automatically
- A visual Knowledge Graph showing how ideas link across your entire library
- A Personal Reading Book — a unique document woven from your highlights, notes, and AI conversations when you finish a book
- Spaced-repetition flashcards (Anki-style SM-2) so you actually retain what you learned
- 5 AI personas with different reading styles
- 8 export formats (CSV, Markdown, HTML, Zotero, APA, MLA, Chicago, BibTeX)
- Reading streaks, daily goals, and progress tracking

**Why we built this:** We kept finishing books and forgetting everything a week later. Highlights and notes would just sit there, unused. read-pal turns passive reading into active conversation — so the ideas actually stick.

Built for students, researchers, and anyone who reads to learn. Open source (MIT).

## First Comment (Maker Comment)

Hey Product Hunt! 👋

I'm the solo developer behind read-pal. I built it because I had a problem: I'd read 2-3 books a month, highlight everything, and then remember almost nothing a week later. My Kindle was a graveyard of yellow text I never revisited.

read-pal takes a different approach. Instead of passively collecting highlights, it turns reading into an active conversation. Your AI companion knows the book, understands context, and actually remembers what you discussed.

The feature I'm most excited about is the **Personal Reading Book** — when you finish a book, read-pal generates a unique document woven from your highlights, notes, AI conversations, and insights. It's like getting a personalized summary, but written through the lens of your own reading experience.

The entire project is open source (MIT) and can be self-hosted. I'd love to hear what you think — what features would make this something you'd use every day?
