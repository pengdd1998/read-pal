## Product Details

**Name:** read-pal

**Tagline:** A friend who reads with you

**Description:**

read-pal is an open-source AI reading companion. Upload any EPUB and read alongside an intelligent friend who explains concepts, asks thoughtful questions, and helps you remember every insight.

Unlike passive e-readers (Kindle) or highlight collectors (Readwise), read-pal actively engages with you while you read. Highlight any passage and ask "why does this matter?" — get an instant, contextual answer without switching tabs.

**Key Features:**
- 5 AI Reading Personas — Choose a companion that matches your style (wise, enthusiastic, challenging, quiet, or practical)
- Personal Knowledge Graph — Ideas connect automatically across all your books
- Memory Books — Beautiful 6-chapter compilations of your reading journey
- Spaced Repetition — SM-2 flashcards from your highlights
- Book Clubs — Read and discuss together
- Smart Export — APA, MLA, Chicago, Zotero, Markdown, JSON

**Why Open Source?**
Your reading data is personal. Self-host on your own server. Export anytime. No lock-in. MIT licensed.

**Categories:** Education, Productivity, Developer Tools, Open Source

**Pricing:** Free (open source, self-hosted)

## First Comment (Maker Comment)

👋 Hi everyone! I built read-pal because I kept forgetting what I read.

I'd highlight passages in Kindle, export to Readwise, and never look at them again. The problem isn't collection — it's comprehension. Nobody was helping me *understand* what I was reading.

read-pal is an AI friend that reads alongside you. It explains passages in context, asks questions that deepen understanding, and builds a knowledge graph connecting everything you've read.

**Tech stack:** Python (FastAPI) + Next.js 14 + LangChain + GLM (Zhipu AI). Docker Compose for one-command deployment. 130+ API endpoints, 320+ tests.

It's completely open source (MIT) and self-hostable. Would love your feedback!
